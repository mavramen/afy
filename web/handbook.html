Here are the information about school procedures

Attendance

California law requires you to call the school when your child does not come to school and to write a note explaining the absence. Please do not send your children if they are ill (have a fever, skin rashes, inflamed eyes, headaches, etc.) School instruction starts promptly at 8:40 a.m. for the middle school and 9:30am for elementary school. We expect all students to arrive on time. If a student is routinely late, the school will contact the parents. Please send a note when your child is to be excused early from school.

Student Drop-Off/Pick-up Instructions

If you are going to drop your child off in the morning, you may do so after 9:15 a.m. at the 12th Avenue white zone only. Please pull all the way to either curbside (being careful not to block the lane of traffic), let your child out, and drive on so other parents can do the same.

Food Service

Students can buy breakfast and hot lunches on a daily basis. Breakfast costs $1.50. Lunch costs $3.00 (reduced or free meals are available). Lunches are prepared by the District at a different location by Revolution Foods.Please complete the Federal Lunch Program forms when they are sent home first week of school. Look for the monthly menu in the Wednesday Red Folder or on the District website You must pre-pay for meals online at MyPaymentsPlus.com

Messages for Students

Students are not allowed to receive phone calls or messages during school. If there is an emergency and you need to get a message to your child, please call the school office. Emergency Information Card is Critical. Include your cell and all day and evening phone numbers so the school can reach you. If you change a phone number, send in a written note to the office. The school must have information to contact you if your child is sick or in case of emergency. Your child's teacher needs to be able to reach you to discuss your child. Positive communication strengthens the Home/School partnership!.
