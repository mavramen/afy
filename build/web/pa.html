Here is the information about Parents Association

The Alice Fong Yu Parents Association consists of a diverse group of parents whose mission is to support the AFY community primarily by raising funds for school enrichment programs, serving as a communication path between parents and staff, and coordinating parent volunteer activities at the school.
The AFYPA is a 501(c)(3) certified nonprofit organization[1]. The Federal Tax ID Number is: 77-0439991. All funds directly support school enrichment programs. All donations are tax deductible to the extent allowed by law.

Meetings

The AFYPA consists of the organization officers (President, Vice President, Treasurer, and Secretary) and approximately a dozen board members. The officers and board members are elected by the AFY parent community to represent their interests. Members are elected for two years, with approximately half of the seats open to election each year.

Cultural Exchange

     Beginning with the year 2000, each year (except 2003) the eighth grade class has made the journey to China to meet their pen pals at our sister-school in Beijing, and to shop in the bustle of Hong Kong. It's the opportunity of a lifetime for them to experience the culture firsthand and put their language skills to the ultimate test. In Beijing, the students stay in the home of a family from the sister school. This intimate experience of life in China is a fitting culmination of study of China, its language, and its culture.

SSC -- School Site Council

     Schools in the San Francisco Unified School District (District) are required to have a School Site Council (SSC) as part of the school’s governance structure. It is made up for a minimum of ten members composed of the principal and peer-elected parents and teachers who serve staggered two-year terms. SSC meetings are conducted monthly during the school year. The SSC follows State guidelines and complies with District policies. The SSC is expected to:

  • Advise the school regarding the planning, budgeting (e.g. allocate funds received from SFUSD), implementation and evaluation of the School Site Plan (also referred to as the “Balanced Scorecard” or “Academic Plan”), including support for those students deemed by student data (e.g., African Americans, English Learners, Special Education students, etc.). The School Site Plan also includes related professional development activities for teachers and staff.
  • Support District SFUSD goals for:
    • Access & Equity (to make social justice a reality)
    • Student Achievement (to engage high achieving and joyful learners)
    • Accountability (to keep District promises to students and families)
  • Gather community input.
  • Emphasize the role of the parent as the first teacher of the child.
  • Take other actions as required to ensure compliance with the California Education Code.
  • Participate in other strategic initiatives as appropriate (e.g. advocacy to preserve the immersion education model, retention of bus routes, applying for awards, participate in annual Enrollment Fairs and City-Wide SSC Summits).

Duties of SSC members include:

  1. Attending monthly council meetings.
  2. Accepting a position as a committee officer (Chair/Secretary) when elected.
  3. Actively participating in workshops and training sessions to increase knowledge of the school site council's purpose and functions.
  4. Regularly reporting SSC proceedings and actions to their own peer groups, and bring back peer group recommendations to the SSC.
  5. Review and approve budgets and school plans (i.e. Balanced Score Card).
